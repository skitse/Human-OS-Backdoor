# Content & Short Video Hooks Playbook

## The Hook Architecture

Every piece of high-performing content has the same structural skeleton:

```
HOOK (0-3 seconds / first sentence)
  └→ Pattern interrupt OR curiosity gap OR identity trigger
  └→ Goal: stop the scroll / make them continue

PROMISE (3-10 seconds / sentence 2-3)
  └→ What will they get from continuing?
  └→ "By the end you'll know X" / "This changed everything"

RE-HOOK (10-30 seconds / paragraph 2)
  └→ Prevent early dropout; deliver first value hit
  └→ Or deepen the curiosity gap

BODY (core value delivery)
  └→ Fulfill promise; build trust; demonstrate expertise

LOOP CLOSE / CTA (ending)
  └→ Resolve open loop OR open new one
  └→ Call to action: share / comment / follow / buy
```

## Short Video Hook Formulas (First 2 Seconds)

### Category 1: Pattern Interrupt Hooks

The formula: Start in an unexpected place.

```
"Most people [do thing X] completely wrong" — challenges consensus
"I just [did shocking thing]" — mid-action start
"What you're about to see can't be unseen" — threshold warning
"[Show surprising result] — here's how I got here" — outcome first
"Stop what you're doing for 60 seconds" — command interrupt
Visual hook: Show unexpected image before explaining (reaction first)
```

### Category 2: Curiosity Gap Hooks

The formula: Make them feel incomplete without watching.

```
"The [profession] secret no one talks about"
"I learned this from [unlikely source]"
"[Number] years ago nobody knew this — now everyone does"
"This [simple thing] made me [dramatic result]"
"Don't watch this if you [don't want result]" — reverse psychology gap
"The reason [common belief] is wrong" — challenge assumption
"I tested [X things] so you don't have to. Results shocked me."
```

### Category 3: Identity/Tribal Hooks

The formula: Make target audience feel the content is FOR THEM specifically.

```
"If you're a [specific identity], this is for you"
"[X type of person] do this — [Y type] doesn't"
"This is only valuable if you [specific situation]"
"[Industry] people will understand this immediately"
"People who [shared belief] already know this"
"If you've ever [specific pain point], listen to this"
```

### Category 4: Stakes Hooks (Fear / Greed)

The formula: Make the cost of not watching feel real.

```
"This mistake cost me [large amount/time/consequence]"
"Everyone who ignored this [suffered X]"
"The opportunity most [target audience] are missing right now"
"In [year], not knowing this will [negative consequence]"
"While you're [doing X], your competitors are [doing Y]"
```

## Retention Architecture (Mid-Content)

### The Loop-and-Reward Pattern

Never deliver one complete thought and then the next. Layer promises:

```
"I'll show you X... but first let me explain Y... [Y delivered]
...and that's why X works. Now here's the part that surprised everyone..."
```

Every 10-15 seconds (video) or every 2 paragraphs (text): **Plant a new hook or deliver on old one.**

### The 3-Act Tension Structure

```
Act 1: STATUS QUO — establish what reader/viewer thinks is true
Act 2: DISRUPTION — introduce the thing that challenges this belief
Act 3: RESOLUTION — provide the new understanding / solution

TRAP: Most creators skip Act 1. Without Act 1, Act 2 has no contrast.
```

### Retention Killers (Avoid)

- Starting with credentials before value ("Hi, I'm [name], I've been doing X for 20 years...")
- Explaining what you're about to do instead of doing it
- Weak hook, strong body (they never reach the body)
- Body without hooks (they leave after getting first value hit)
- Abrupt ending without loop closure or new loop opening

## Virality Engineering

### The Virality Equation (Jonah Berger's STEPPS)

```
SOCIAL CURRENCY: Does sharing make me look good?
TRIGGERS: Does environment remind people to share?
EMOTION: Does it evoke high-arousal emotion (awe, anger, fear, laughter)?
PUBLIC: Is behavior visible to others?
PRACTICAL VALUE: Does it provide real useful information?
STORIES: Is there a narrative that carries the message?
```

**High-virality content hits 3+ of these simultaneously.**

### Emotion → Virality Mapping

High-arousal emotions drive sharing; low-arousal suppress it:

```
VIRAL → Awe, Anger, Anxiety, Fear (high arousal, negative or positive)
NOT VIRAL → Sadness, Contentment, Serenity (low arousal)
```

**Implication**: Calm, reassuring content doesn't go viral. Content that makes people feel SOMETHING URGENT does.

### The Meme Mechanics

A meme = Shareable vessel that carries an idea through social contagion.

**Anatomy of a viral meme**:
```
1. RECOGNIZABLE FORMAT: Familiar template lowers cognitive resistance
2. SURPRISING CONTENT: Unexpected content in familiar format = contrast
3. IDENTITY SIGNAL: Sharing it says something about the sharer
4. LOW EFFORT TO ENGAGE: One-tap reshare; no friction
5. INCOMPLETE REFERENCE: Inside joke structure ("if you know, you know")
```

**Meme engineering process**:
```
Step 1: Identify existing high-traction format in target community
Step 2: Map core idea to format structure
Step 3: Add insider element that signals community membership
Step 4: Make sharing it an identity signal, not just forwarding
Step 5: A/B test opening line — meme lives or dies in first impression
```

## Copy Architecture

### AIDA+ Framework (Modern Version)

```
ATTENTION: Hook that stops the reader
INTEREST: Why should they care? (speaks to their identity/pain)
DESIRE: What does the solution look like/feel like?
ACTION: Specific, low-friction next step
MOMENTUM: Give them a reason to share/forward
```

### High-Converting CTAs

Principles:
- Specific > Vague ("Download the 5-minute guide" > "Learn more")
- Value-led > Action-led ("Get your free template" > "Subscribe")
- Loss-framed > Gain-framed for re-engagement ("Don't miss tomorrow's..." > "Join for...")
- Minimal cognitive load: one CTA per piece of content

### Subject Line / Thumbnail Formulas

```
"[Result] without [common obstacle]"
"The [time frame] [achievement]"
"[Number] [things] that [target group] gets wrong"
"[Unusual source] taught me [valuable lesson]"
"You're [doing X] — that's why [Y isn't working]"
"[Surprising number]% of [respected group] does this"
```
