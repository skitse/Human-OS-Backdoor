## Retention Diagnostics by Time Window

### 0-3 Seconds

**Typical failure**
- intro ritual
- logo first
- context before payoff
- visually flat opening

**What usually works**
- outcome first
- strong contrast or movement
- a specific claim
- a visible unresolved question

**Rule**
- begin with the most watch-worthy frame, not the politest frame

### 3-15 Seconds

**Typical failure**
- viewer understands topic but not reward
- setup keeps expanding without a concrete promise

**What usually works**
- state what the viewer will get
- narrow the question
- deliver one small value hit early

**Rule**
- by 15 seconds, the viewer should know why continuing is worth it

### 20-50% Through

**Typical failure**
- one-note pacing
- no structural signal of progress
- the viewer predicts the rest too easily

**What usually works**
- re-hook with a new angle, reveal, or conflict
- shift scene, energy, or example
- summarize and reopen the loop

**Rule**
- do not let the middle become a flat explanation block

### Final 20%

**Typical failure**
- payoff already happened, so the tail feels optional
- CTA arrives after energy is gone

**What usually works**
- compress the most useful synthesis near the end
- close the main loop clearly
- place CTA after payoff, but while momentum is still high

**Rule**
- endings should feel like resolution, not administrative cleanup

## The 4 Structural Jobs of a Strong Video

### 1. Hook

Stop the scroll.

Good hook ingredients:

- obvious relevance
- prediction break
- concrete stake
- visual specificity

### 2. Promise

Tell viewers what continuing gets them.

Good promise ingredients:

- one main outcome
- short time horizon
- no vague "let's talk about..."

### 3. Re-Hook

Renew attention before the first fatigue drop.

Good re-hook ingredients:

- a new example
- an unexpected turn
- a more specific payoff
- a stronger question

### 4. Payoff

Deliver the thing the video implicitly sold.

Good payoff ingredients:

- clear answer
- memorable phrasing
- next-step relevance

## Practical Patterns

### Outcome-First Opening

Best for:

- tutorials
- case studies
- transformation stories

Pattern:

- show the result
- then explain how it happened

### Question-First Opening

Best for:

- educational content
- analysis
- myth-busting

Pattern:

- ask a question the audience already half-cares about
- avoid questions so broad they feel generic

### Conflict Pivot Midway

Best for:

- commentary
- essays
- narrative videos

Pattern:

- "This is where the obvious explanation breaks."
- "Here's the part most people miss."

### Compression Ending

Best for:

- knowledge content
- conversion-oriented content

Pattern:

- restate the core takeaway in one clean sentence
- immediately attach the CTA or next action

---

## Common Failure Modes

- opening with identity or greeting before value
- explaining the structure instead of starting the content
- repeating the same mechanism label instead of changing the viewing experience
- saving all value for the end
- using a CTA that is disconnected from the emotional state of the ending

---

## Review Checklist

1. Is the first frame the strongest frame?
2. Is the promise clear within 15 seconds?
3. Does the middle contain at least one real re-hook?
4. Is the ending still adding value, not just wrapping up?
5. Could the viewer explain the main payoff in one sentence after watching?
